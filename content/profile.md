## Who Josh is

Joshua Agarwal is a professional engineer (P.Eng) and MBA candidate at the Wharton School (Class of 2028). Before Wharton, he spent four years as a Facilities Engineer in Enbridge's New Energy Technologies group in Calgary, working on first-of-a-kind low-carbon infrastructure — hydrogen, carbon capture and storage, and renewable natural gas.

His professional focus is the gap between design and operation: many technically sound energy projects stall not because the engineering fails, but because risk tolerance, capital allocation, and stakeholder alignment do. He works at the intersection of engineering, operations, risk, and organizational readiness.

## What he did in energy

- **Operational readiness for new asset classes** — helped shape Enbridge's early operational-readiness approach for low-carbon assets, coordinating cross-functional workstreams (integrity, control systems, operations, regulatory, emergency management) without formal authority over any of them.
- **In-house technical and economic analysis** — moved conceptual engineering studies in-house (process modeling, early economics, risk framing), reducing reliance on external consultants and accelerating investment decisions.
- **AI and analytics tooling** — built data-driven decision tools adopted by operating teams, including probabilistic models supporting multimillion-dollar equipment-sparing decisions; an early advocate for moving teams beyond spreadsheet-driven analysis.
- **External knowledge capture** — represented the company at clean-energy conferences on hydrogen, carbon management, and alternative fuels, translating external technology signals into internal guidance. Known for separating technology maturity from hype.

## Leadership outside work

- **Young Professionals in Energy (Calgary):** joined as a volunteer, chaired the flagship Future Leaders Dinner (120+ attendees, sold out), helped a divided board align on strategy, and was unanimously elected 2026 chapter President. The chapter delivered its strongest year: twelve events, new sponsors, measurable membership growth.
- **Avatar Innovations:** led a four-person clean-tech venture team through a nine-month energy venture studio, navigating a major pivot and winning the program's GRIT Award for resilience and execution. The experience shaped his conviction that clean-energy scaling problems are execution and capital problems, not just technology problems.

## Where he's headed

Josh is pursuing energy and infrastructure strategy — the work of helping leaders make risk-informed decisions about what actually gets built in the energy transition. Long term, he aims to build or lead a climate-infrastructure platform: an organization that aligns technology, capital, and operational execution to scale low-carbon assets faster than incumbents.
