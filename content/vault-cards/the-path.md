title: "The path — engineer to Wharton"
---

Josh is a chemical engineer (P.Eng) who spent eight years in Canadian energy — pipelines, capital projects, and most recently new energy technologies and AI adoption at a major infrastructure company — before betting on himself with a full-time MBA at the Wharton School (Class of 2028).

The application story he tells straight: no banking or consulting pedigree, a GMAT that started at a 485 diagnostic and took three rounds and hundreds of after-work hours to drag to a 655. He got into Wharton with over $200,000 in scholarship offers across the schools he applied to — by building the entire strategy around his non-traditional background instead of hiding it. Alongside work he served as VP of a young professionals energy organization, led community events, and co-founded initiatives in the energy transition space. He now coaches MBA applicants on the same playbook.

How he operates, from the principles he actually keeps in his vault (see [[Values & Principles]]): focus beats novelty ("eliminate shiny object syndrome"); speak less, listen more (his hardest one — see his blind spots); skills compound, so build slowly and deliberately; treat every role as a calling, not a job. His faith is the foundation under all of it — something he writes about openly in his published essays.

Where he's pointed: the intersection of energy and AI — strategy and deployment work where his technical depth is the moat. The specific vehicle (consulting, a lab, an operator role) he treats as an open, deliberate choice rather than a default.
