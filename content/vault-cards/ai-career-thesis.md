title: "The deployment gap — Josh's AI thesis"
---

Josh's core thesis on AI: the bottleneck is no longer the models — it's the last mile. Frontier capability is outrunning enterprise adoption, and the scarce skill is translating what these tools can do into workflows a real organization actually runs. He calls this the deployment gap (see [[The Deployment Gap - My AI Career Thesis]] in the vault), and he wrote about it publicly after OpenAI committed roughly $14 billion to industrializing exactly that: putting engineers inside customers instead of advising from outside.

He didn't reach the thesis from headlines. He lives in the gap — eight years in Canadian energy, most recently driving AI adoption inside a large energy infrastructure company, where he watched capable tools sit unused because nobody combined business judgment with technical fluency in the same chair.

The thesis is also honestly contested inside his own vault. A Wharton alum who now operates at an AI startup pushed back: deployment is real demand today, but it's a gap that closes as technology matures — the durable layers are compute infrastructure and foundational models (see [[World Models]]). Josh logged the counterpoint and downgraded his own confidence accordingly. His current synthesis: deployment is the right entry point and plays to his strengths, but the lasting moat is domain depth — for him, energy — because that's the context models can't fake.

What that means for his path: stay close to real AI implementation, keep the energy edge sharp, and treat prestige paths as deliberate trades rather than defaults.
