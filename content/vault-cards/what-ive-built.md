Josh's rule: AI that doesn't ship into a real workflow is a demo. What's actually running:

A one-tap capture pipeline. Share any YouTube video from his phone and thirty seconds later a structured, AI-formatted note lands in his knowledge vault — title, summary, key ideas, tags, source. Built with an Android automation tool, a transcript API, and Claude. Five hours of debugging, permanent leverage. He wrote the build up publicly.

A gas turbine sparing simulator. A decision-support web app for a real compressor fleet: eight field engines backed by two or three spares, where running out means a station goes offline. It optimizes a 10-year maintenance schedule against real constraints (overhaul intervals, shop turnaround times, utilization), then runs 500 Monte Carlo simulations to quantify the probability of a zero-spare event — informing a multi-million-dollar engine purchase decision. Public on his GitHub.

The second brain itself. An Obsidian vault with automated maintenance: scheduled agents that scan for open follow-ups, generate a prioritized dashboard, sync time-sensitive items to his to-do list, and produce weekly briefings. Plus a local transcription pipeline that turns recorded mentor conversations into synthesized, cross-linked notes. The vault is explorable in 3D on this site at /brain.

This website. Including the chatbot — the curated public interface to the vault.

The pattern across all of it: he doesn't build novelty, he builds leverage. Capture once, structure it, connect it, let it compound.
