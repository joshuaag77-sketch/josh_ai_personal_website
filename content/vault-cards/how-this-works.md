title: "How this vault works"
---

This chatbot is the public interface to Josh's second brain — a personal knowledge system he built in Obsidian. The real vault lives on his machine: over a hundred interlinked notes, hundreds of connections, dozens of people mapped, synthesized from years of mentor conversations, career assessments, articles, books, and his own builds. He captures everything as plain markdown files he owns forever, then uses AI to read across all of it and find patterns no single note shows. A live export of the graph's structure is explorable in 3D at /brain on this site.

What the chatbot can see is a deliberately curated public slice — cards Josh wrote and approved for exactly this purpose, plus his published articles and the note titles of the public graph. His private reflections, journal, relationships, faith life, work specifics, and the names of people in his network stay on his machine. That's by design: the private vault is the engine, this is the showroom.

Why it exists: Josh believes the bottleneck in AI isn't the models — it's connecting them to real context. This site is his proof of concept. The same architecture (capture everything, connect it, let AI synthesize) is what he builds professionally.
